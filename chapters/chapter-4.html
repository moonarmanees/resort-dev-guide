Chapter 4: Feasibility, Site Selection & Concept Development

This stage lays the groundwork for the entire project. A comprehensive feasibility study and a well-defined concept are crucial for attracting investors and guiding the design process.

Feasibility Study Components

  • Market Analysis: Target demographic, competitor analysis, and pricing strategy.
  • Financial Projections: Estimated development costs, operational expenses, and revenue forecasts (ADR, Occupancy, RevPAR).
  • Site Analysis: Assessment of the islandâ€™s topography, marine biodiversity, accessibility, and potential environmental constraints.
  • Risk Assessment: Identification of potential financial, operational, and environmental risks.

Concept Development

The concept defines the resort's identity and unique selling proposition (USP). Key considerations include:

  • Resort Theme: (e.g., luxury, eco-friendly, family-oriented, wellness-focused).
  • Accommodation Mix: Number and type of villas (beach, overwater).
  • Amenities and Facilities: Restaurants, spa, dive center, recreational activities.
  • Sustainability Goals: Commitments to renewable energy, waste management, and conservation.
