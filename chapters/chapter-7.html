Chapter 7: Contractor Procurement and Management

Selecting the right contractor is critical to ensuring the quality, cost, and schedule of the construction phase. A transparent and competitive procurement process is highly recommended.

Procurement Process

  1. Prequalification: Interested contractors are vetted based on their experience, financial stability, and technical capabilities.
  2. Tendering (Bidding): Prequalified contractors are invited to submit bids based on the detailed construction documents.
  3. Bid Evaluation: Bids are evaluated based on price, proposed schedule, methodology, and compliance with the tender requirements. (See Appendix F for a template).
  4. Negotiation and Award: The preferred contractor is selected, and contract terms are finalized. A Letter of Award (LOA) is issued.

Contract Types

  • Lump Sum (Fixed Price): The contractor agrees to complete the project for a predetermined price. Best for projects with a clearly defined scope.
  • Cost-Plus: The owner pays the contractor for the actual costs of labor and materials, plus an agreed-upon fee for profit and overhead. More flexible but carries more financial risk for the owner.
  • Design-Build: A single contract is awarded for both the design and construction of the project.
