Chapter 2: Project Team Assembly and Key Roles

Assembling a skilled and experienced team is fundamental to the success of any resort development project. The core team should be established at the earliest stage to ensure alignment and effective decision-making.

Core Project Team Roles

  • Owner/Developer Representative: The primary decision-maker who provides project oversight and funding.
  • Project Manager: Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, coordinating all parties, and ensuring the project stays on schedule and within budget.
  • Lead Architect/Designer: Develops the master plan and architectural vision for the resort.
  • Lead Engineer (Structural, MEP): Oversees all engineering aspects, ensuring structural integrity and functionality of mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems.
  • Environmental Consultant: Manages the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and ensures compliance with environmental regulations.
  • Legal Counsel: Advises on contracts, permits, and all legal matters related to the development.

Extended Team Members (As Needed)

  • Quantity Surveyor
  • Interior Designer
  • Landscape Architect
  • Marine Biologist (for coral reef management)
  • Resort Operations Consultant
