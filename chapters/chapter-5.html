Chapter 5: Design and Engineering Development

The design phase translates the approved concept into detailed architectural and engineering plans. This phase is iterative and requires close collaboration between the architect, engineers, and project manager.

Design Stages

  1. Conceptual Design: Initial sketches and massing studies to establish the overall layout and aesthetic.
  2. Schematic Design: Development of floor plans, elevations, and sections.
  3. Design Development: Refinement of the design with detailed specifications for materials, finishes, and systems.
  4. Construction Documents: Final, detailed drawings and specifications used for bidding and construction.

Key Engineering Considerations

  • Structural Design: Ensuring all structures can withstand local weather conditions, including wind and wave loads.
  • MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing): Design of power generation (often diesel generators with solar integration), desalination plants for fresh water, wastewater treatment systems, and HVAC.
  • Coastal Engineering: Design of jetties, breakwaters, and other marine structures to minimize environmental impact.
