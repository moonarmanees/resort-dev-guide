Chapter 3: Regulatory Framework – Permit Acquisition Process & Regulatory Submissions

Navigating the Maldivian regulatory landscape is a critical path item. A thorough understanding of the required permits and a systematic approach to submissions are essential to avoid costly delays.

Key Regulatory Bodies

  • Ministry of Tourism: The primary governing body for resort development and operations.
  • Environmental Protection Agency (EPA): Oversees environmental regulations and approves the EIA.
  • Ministry of National Planning, Housing and Infrastructure: Approves building permits and land use plans.
  • Maldives Customs Service: Manages the import of construction materials and equipment.

Essential Permits and Clearances

  1. Land Lease Agreement: Secured from the Ministry of Tourism.
  2. Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Approval: A mandatory requirement before any physical work can commence.
  3. Building Permits: For all structures, including overwater villas, jetties, and back-of-house facilities.
  4. Import/Export Licenses: For construction materials and equipment.
  5. Resort Operating License: The final approval required to open the resort to guests.
