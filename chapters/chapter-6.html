Chapter 6: Project Financing Strategies

Securing adequate financing is one of the most significant hurdles in resort development. A robust financial plan and a compelling investment proposal are essential.

Common Financing Sources

  • Equity Financing: Funds provided by the owners or private investors in exchange for an ownership stake.
  • Debt Financing: Loans from commercial banks or development finance institutions (DFIs). This often requires a significant equity contribution and a detailed project plan.
  • Mezzanine Financing: A hybrid of debt and equity financing that is subordinate to senior debt.
  • Presales of Villas: Selling long-term leases on private villas within the resort to generate upfront capital.

The Investment Proposal

A comprehensive investment package should include:

  • A detailed Executive Summary.
  • The full Feasibility Study (from Chapter 4).
  • Concept and Schematic Designs.
  • Projected construction budget and timeline.
  • Biographies of the key project team members.
  • A clear exit strategy for investors.
